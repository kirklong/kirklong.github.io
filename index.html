  Research Experience

Modelling the effects of disk-winds in accretion disks

Project led by Prof. Jason Dexter - August 2020 - Current

Performed simplified calculations that show how observations of the broadline region in quasars could be a result of a thin disk with a "wind" viewed at high inclinations, as opposed to the standard model of assuming a puffy disk of particle clouds viewed closer to face on, with potential implications for the inferred masses of central black holes.

Centroid/radii comparison plot

Identifying accreting x-ray binaries

Project led by Prof. Daryl Macomb - April 2019 - July 2020

Analyzed archival data from the CHANDRA and XMM-Newton observatories to search for both new pulsars and those with changing periods, indicating accretion.

Folded LCs for potential pulsar pair
